Vienna Philharmonic – Summer Night Concert 2026

image

Concert image

The Vienna Philharmonic's annual Summer Night Concert returns to the magnificent gardens of Schönbrunn Palace on June 21, 2026. This free open-air event, broadcast live to over 50 countries, is one of the most anticipated musical evenings of the year.

This year's programme, curated by guest conductor Gustavo Dudamel, spans three centuries of orchestral music — from the grandeur of Beethoven's Seventh Symphony to the romantic sweep of Dvořák's New World Symphony, with a special tribute to Austrian composer Franz Schubert marking his 229th anniversary.

The concert takes place at dusk against the Baroque backdrop of the palace, with the gardens illuminated in a spectacular light installation designed by lighting artist Felice Varini. Attendance is free of charge and open to the public; however, reserved seating areas are available through Ticketline.

"Music under the stars at Schönbrunn is one of those rare experiences that reminds us why we live for moments like these."

— Gustavo Dudamel, Guest Conductor

Gates open at 7:00 PM and the concert begins at 9:30 PM. Visitors are encouraged to bring blankets and picnic baskets to enjoy the grounds before the performance. Limited premium seating with catering packages is available — book now to secure your spot.
